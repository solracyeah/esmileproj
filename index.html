    Composite Restoration

    Composite restoration is a technique in which a tooth-colored resin material is used to restore and/or reshape the teeth. The strength of these plastic materials is approximately one-third that of natural tooth structure, so there is a risk of chipping.

    Invisalign

    Invisalign is the most popular and widely-known clear aligner. Invisalign aligners are made out of SmartTrack® material that is specifically engineered for the Invisalign system. It is the #1 aligner in the industry.

    Braces

    Corrects misaligned teeth, overcrowding, gaps, and bite issues (overbite, underbite, crossbite).

    Tooth Extraction

    he removal of a tooth, often performed when it is severely decayed, damaged, or impacted. This relieves pain, prevents infection, or makes room for orthodontic treatment.

    Gold Restoration

    Gold restorations are incredibly strong and resistant to wear, making them ideal for molars where chewing forces are highest. Our gold restorations are custom-made to fit perfectly, reducing gaps that could lead to decay or gum disease.

    Dental Crown

    Repairs minor chips, cracks, or gaps and improves the shape or size of teeth. It’s a more affordable and quicker alternative to veneers for small corrections.

    Palatal Extenders

    A device used to widen the upper jaw by applying gentle pressure over time. It is often used in children to create space for permanent teeth. It corrects narrow arches, crossbites, and overcrowding.

    Bone Grafting

    Restores bone density and structure to support implants or correct bone loss from injury or disease.

    Veneers

    Dental veneers are wafer-thin, custom-made shells of tooth-colored materials designed to cover the front surface of teeth to change your appearance.

    Dental Bridges

    A prosthetic device used to replace one or more missing teeth by anchoring an artificial tooth to adjacent natural teeth or implants.

    Temporary Anchorage Devices (TADs)

    Mini titanium screws temporarily placed in the jawbone to provide a stable anchor for moving teeth.

    Maxilloficial Surgery

    A broad category addressing severe facial injuries, congenital abnormalities, or jaw deformities. It restores function and aesthetics to the face and jaw after trauma or for reconstructive purposes.
